About Teddy Bears

The teddy bear was born in 1902, named after President Theodore "Teddy" Roosevelt. The story goes that during a hunting trip, Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear that had been tied to a tree, deeming it unsportsmanlike. This act of compassion inspired a political cartoon and eventually led to the creation of the beloved teddy bear.

Today, teddy bears continue to be one of the most cherished toys worldwide. The provide comfort to children, serve as collectibles for adults, and represent love, friendship, and security across all cultures.
